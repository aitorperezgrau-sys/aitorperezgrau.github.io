Analytical Projects

🚀 Flight Dynamics Faraday Rocketry

Skybreaker — 6-DOF Flight Analysis & Trajectory Optimisation

Independent technical analysis of Skybreaker, Faraday Rocketry UPV's solid rocket that won the EuRoC 2025 3 km category. Using Python and RocketPy, the report validates the vehicle's performance envelope and determines the optimal launch heading to maximise apogee.

  • 6-DOF Simulation — RocketPy modelling of variable mass inertia, thrust curves, static margins and Mach-dependent drag coefficients.
  • Wind Compensation — Weathercocking analysis against specific wind vectors (Y: −5.76 m/s, X: 4.39 m/s) to optimise launch rail heading to 145°.
  • Result — Optimal apogee of 2,837.5 m achieved with safe stability parameters — documented in a 14-page technical report.

🛰️ Orbital Mechanics UPV Coursework

LEO–GEO Hohmann Transfer — In-Space Propulsion Feasibility Analysis

A MATLAB feasibility study modelling propellant-mass requirements for a bidirectional LEO↔GEO Hohmann transfer (Δv = 7,864 × 10³ m/s), benchmarking four propulsion technologies and drawing quantitative conclusions about the limits of current in-space propulsion.

  • Mass Modelling — Propellant mass vs specific impulse computed for Cold Gas CO₂, Hydrazine, LOX/CH₄ and LOX/H₂ across 7 orders of magnitude.
  • Data Visualisation — Annotated constraint overlays and log-scale comparative plots for clear cross-technology trade-off communication.
  • Conclusion — No current single-stage technology meets mission mass constraints, directly motivating the need for advanced green propulsion systems.

✈️ Aviation Safety UPV Coursework

Commercial Aviation Safety Cost Analysis

A parameterised Excel financial model covering six commercial aircraft types (A320 to B787), linking operational metrics to financial variables to compute standardised costs of aircraft loss, fatalities, maintenance, and acquisition.

  • Scope — Six aircraft types (A320 → B787), combining RPK, flight cycles, hull loss rates, acquisition cost, and statistical value of human life.
  • Finding — Maintenance cost alone is an insufficient safety proxy given EASA/ICAO standardisation and the ~75% human-error share of accidents.
